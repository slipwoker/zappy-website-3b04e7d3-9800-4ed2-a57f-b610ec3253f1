How Spinal Decompression May Help Chronic Low Back Pain

June 15, 2025 · 5 min read

Chronic low back pain is one of the most common reasons people seek care at our Hillsdale practice. It can make everyday activities — bending to tie your shoes, lifting a grandchild, or simply sitting through a meeting — feel like a challenge. For many patients, the frustration isn’t just the pain itself, but the feeling that they’ve tried everything and nothing has offered lasting relief.

At Howard Kessler, DC, we see patients from every walk of life: teenagers navigating sports injuries, weekend warriors pushing their limits, busy professionals hunched over desks, and seniors determined to stay active well into their golden years. Spinal decompression is one of the gentle, non-invasive therapies we offer that can make a meaningful difference for people dealing with persistent back pain.

What Is Spinal Decompression?

Spinal decompression is a non-surgical treatment designed to relieve pressure on the spinal discs and nerves. It works by gently stretching the spine, creating negative pressure within the discs. This negative pressure can help retract herniated or bulging disc material, allowing oxygen, water, and nutrient-rich fluids to flow back into the disc and promote healing.

Unlike surgical decompression, the non-surgical approach we use is comfortable, controlled, and performed while you lie fully clothed on an Excite Medical DRX spinal decompression table. Most patients find the experience deeply relaxing — some even fall asleep during their session.

Who Can Benefit from Spinal Decompression?

Because our practice serves such a broad patient base throughout Bergen County, we’ve seen spinal decompression help a remarkably diverse group of people. It may be appropriate for individuals experiencing:

Herniated or bulging discs that cause radiating pain down the leg, often called sciatica. Degenerative disc disease, where the cushioning between vertebrae has worn down over time. Posterior facet syndrome, a condition affecting the small joints at the back of the spine. And even for some patients who have been told surgery is their only option, decompression can offer a conservative path worth exploring first.

We always begin with a thorough evaluation — including a detailed health history and, when appropriate, imaging — to determine whether spinal decompression is right for you. Every treatment plan is tailored to the individual, because we know a 17-year-old athlete and an 85-year-old gardener have very different bodies and goals.

What Does a Session Feel Like?

One of the questions we hear most often is, “Will it hurt?” The answer is almost always no. Spinal decompression is gentle by design. You’ll feel a slow, rhythmic stretching sensation in your lower back — never sudden, never forceful. The Excite Medical DRX table is programmed to cycle through periods of gentle tension and relaxation, allowing your muscles to stay at ease throughout the treatment.

A typical session lasts about 30 to 45 minutes, and many patients notice a sense of relief even after their first visit. That said, lasting results usually come from a series of treatments combined with supportive care like chiropractic adjustments, targeted exercises, and lifestyle guidance.

A Community-Centered Approach to Back Pain

What sets our practice apart isn’t just the technology — it’s the philosophy. We believe that healthcare should feel personal, not transactional. When you walk through our doors at 230 Broadway, you’re not a case number. You’re a neighbor, a parent, a person with a life you want to get back to fully.

We take time to listen, to explain your options in plain language, and to celebrate every milestone with you — whether that’s playing a full round of golf without pain or simply sleeping through the night for the first time in months. Chronic low back pain doesn’t have to be your new normal, and you don’t have to face it alone.

If you’ve been wondering whether spinal decompression might be right for you, we invite you to reach out. Let’s have a conversation — no pressure, no obligation — just honest, compassionate guidance from a team that truly cares about helping you feel your best.
