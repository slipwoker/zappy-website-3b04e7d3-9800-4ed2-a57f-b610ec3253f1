How Spinal Decompression May Help Chronic Low Back Pain

June 15, 2025 · 5 min read

That deep, radiating ache traveling from your lower back down through your buttock and leg can stop you in your tracks. Sciatica affects millions of people every year, yet the path to relief often feels confusing. At Howard Kessler, DC, we believe understanding your symptoms is the first step toward reclaiming your comfort and mobility. Here is what you need to know about sciatica—and when conservative chiropractic care may be the right choice for you.

What Sciatica Actually Feels Like

Sciatica is not a condition itself but a symptom of an underlying issue involving the sciatic nerve, the largest nerve in the body. Patients often describe it as a shooting or burning pain that starts in the lower spine and travels through the hip, buttock, and down one leg. Some people experience numbness, tingling, or a sensation like pins and needles. The discomfort can range from a mild ache to a sharp, almost electrical jolt that makes standing or walking difficult. Recognizing these sensations early helps you make informed decisions about seeking care.

Common Triggers and Risk Factors

Sciatica can flare up for a variety of reasons. A herniated disc pressing on the nerve root is among the most frequent causes. Spinal stenosis, or a narrowing of the spinal canal, can also create pressure that radiates down the leg. Less common but still significant triggers include muscle spasms in the piriformis muscle deep in the buttock, or even pregnancy-related changes in pelvic alignment. Risk factors such as prolonged sitting, heavy lifting with poor mechanics, and age-related wear and tear can all increase your likelihood of developing sciatic pain.

When to Consider Conservative Chiropractic Care

Many people assume surgery is inevitable once they hear the word "sciatica," but the reality is far more hopeful. Conservative, non-invasive treatments are effective for the vast majority of patients. If your pain is manageable, if you are not experiencing progressive leg weakness, and if you can still perform most daily activities with some modification, chiropractic care is an excellent first step. Our approach focuses on gentle spinal adjustments to restore proper alignment, reduce nerve irritation, and create the space your body needs to heal naturally.

Red Flags That Require Immediate Attention

While conservative care is appropriate for most people, certain symptoms should prompt an urgent medical evaluation. Sudden loss of bladder or bowel control, progressive numbness in the saddle area, or severe weakness that causes your foot to drag when walking are all signs that require immediate attention. These "red flag" symptoms, though rare, indicate a more serious compression that may need emergency intervention. If you are ever unsure, we encourage you to err on the side of caution and seek a prompt assessment.

What to Expect During Your First Visit

Walking into a new healthcare practice can feel intimidating, so we have designed our initial consultation to be unhurried and conversational. You will sit down with Dr. Kessler to discuss your symptoms, health history, and personal goals. A thorough physical and neurological exam helps pinpoint the source of your discomfort. If imaging such as X-rays is needed, we coordinate that seamlessly. By the end of your first visit, you will have a clear understanding of what is causing your pain and a personalized plan to move forward. Our patients range from ten years old to one hundred, and every plan is built around the individual, not just the diagnosis.

Supporting Your Recovery at Home

Healing from sciatica is a partnership. Alongside your in-office adjustments, small daily habits can make a meaningful difference. Gentle stretching, particularly for the hamstrings and lower back, helps maintain flexibility. Applying ice during the first 48 hours of a flare-up can reduce inflammation, while heat later encourages blood flow and relaxation. Mindful posture—whether you are sitting at a desk, driving, or lifting groceries—protects the progress you make between visits. We will guide you through these strategies so you feel empowered, not overwhelmed.

Sciatica can feel isolating, but you do not have to navigate it alone. At our practice on Broadway in Hillsdale, we have had the privilege of helping Bergen County neighbors of all ages and lifestyles find relief without surgery or heavy medication. If you are ready to explore a conservative path forward, we are here to listen and support you every step of the way.
