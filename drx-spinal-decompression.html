Frequently Asked Questions

Understanding Spinal Decompression

Get clear answers about DRX spinal decompression therapy for herniated discs, so you can make informed decisions about your spine health.

What is DRX spinal decompression and how does it work?

DRX spinal decompression is a non-surgical therapy that gently stretches the spine to relieve pressure on herniated or bulging discs. By creating negative pressure within the disc, it can help retract the herniated material, promote nutrient and oxygen exchange, and support the body's natural healing process—all without medication or invasive procedures.

Am I a candidate for spinal decompression therapy?

Most people with herniated discs, degenerative disc disease, or sciatica are good candidates. During your initial consultation at our Hillsdale office, Dr. Kessler will review your medical history, perform a thorough examination, and determine if DRX decompression is right for you. We treat patients of all ages—from teenagers to active seniors.

Is the treatment painful?

Not at all. Most patients find DRX spinal decompression comfortable and even relaxing. The therapy uses precise, computer-controlled tension that is customized to your body. Many people listen to music, read, or simply rest during their session. You may feel a gentle stretch, but the process should never cause pain.

How many sessions will I need?

A typical treatment plan involves 20 to 28 sessions over 6 to 8 weeks, with each session lasting about 30 to 45 minutes. However, every spine is different. Dr. Kessler will create a personalized schedule based on your specific condition, lifestyle, and how your body responds to the early sessions.

Are the results long-lasting?

Yes, when combined with the supportive care and lifestyle guidance we provide. DRX decompression addresses the root cause of disc pain rather than just masking symptoms. To protect your results, we also offer strengthening exercises, posture education, and periodic check-ins that fit into your daily routine—whether you are a weekend athlete or a busy professional.
